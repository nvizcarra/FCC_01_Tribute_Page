J. R. R. Tolkien

"Faithless is he that says farewell when the road darkens."

J. R. R. Tolkien time line:

  • 1892: Born John Ronald Reuel Tolkien in Bloemfontein, Orange Free State, South Africa
  • 1896: Father dies and Tolkien's family moves to Sarehole, U.K., a small town outside Birmingham; beings receiving education from his mother in Latin, French, and German.
  • 1915: Serves during World War I as a second lieutenant with the Lancashire Fusiliers.
  • 1916: Marries Edith Bratt and leaves for France, where he participates in the Battle of the Somme; stricken with trench fever five months later and returns home to England.
  • 1917: Begins writing The Silmarilion: The Myths and Legends of Middle-earth.
  • 1925: Moves from Leeds, U.K., to Oxford University, where he becomes Rawlinson and Bosworth Professor of Anglo-saxon (Old English).
  • 1937: Releases The Hobbit on September 21 and sees it sell out by Christmas (it is eventually translated into 31 languages); begins to write The Lord of the Rings in December.
  • 1939-1945: World War II
  • 1954-1956: Releases the three Lord of the Rings books, which are eventually translated into 21 languages.
  • 1973: Dies at 81 (The Silmarillion was published in 1977, and the 12 volumes of The History of Middle-earth are published between 1984 and 1996.)
